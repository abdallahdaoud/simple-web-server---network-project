1942

Mohammad Ali (Cassius Marcellus Clay) was born on January 17, 1942, in Louisville, Kentucky, to a middle-class black American family. His father was a methodist, but his mother raised him and his brother as a baptist.
1954
He went to the world of boxing - by coincidence - while he was still 12 years old, and he won several titles at the local and national levels while he was under 18, and won the gold medal in the Olympic Games in Rome, in 1960, in the light heavyweight category.
In October 1960, Cassius moved into the professional world, where he fought for the next three years 19 fights in which he all won, 15 of them by knockout.
1964
In February, he also achieved a big surprise by defeating world boxing champion, Sonny Liston, and winning the World Heavyweight Professional Boxing Championship for the first time, setting a record as the youngest boxer (22 years) to achieve the championship title in this field.
1965
In May, he converted from Christianity to Islam, changed his original name and surname, and called himself Mohammad Ali.
1967
He refused to join the military forces because of his religious belief and opposition to the American war on Vietnam, but the US government arrested him for evading military service. He was withdrawn the championship title and he was suspended from playing for four years.
1971
The United States Supreme Court overturned his conviction on technical grounds, and Mohammad Ali became a symbol of the counterculture of the younger generation of Americans.
In March, he faced the boxer Joe Frazier in a match called the "Battle of the Century", and he lost for the first time by a unanimous decision by the match officials. After the match, both boxers were transferred to hospital due to the ferocity of the fighting.
1974
He won the World Boxing Championship for the second time after defeating the boxer George Foreman in a match in Kinshasa, capital of the Congo.
1978
He won his third World Boxing Championship title after defeating the boxer Leon Spinks.
1981
He played the last game of his professional career and lost it to the boxer Trevor Berbick by the decision of all the officials after the tenth round.
His illness did not prevent him from participating in many humanitarian actions, as he was awarded the Medal of Freedom in honor of him for his charitable humanitarian aid. He also received a Global Leadership Award from an association that supports the United Nations activity for his leadership role in supporting humanitarian causes.
1984
He began showing symptoms of Parkinson's disease.
2016
On June 4, it was announced that the boxing champion Mohammad Ali Clay had died in Phoenix Hospital, in Arizona, USA, at the age of 74, after a long struggle with Parkinson's disease.
